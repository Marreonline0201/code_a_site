## What Do the Numbers on Mineral Water Labels Mean?

When you pick up a bottle of mineral water, the label often lists a series of minerals and their concentrations in milligrams per liter (mg/L). Understanding these numbers can help you choose the right water for your health goals.

### Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

TDS is the single most important number on a mineral water label. It represents the total amount of dissolved minerals in the water, measured in mg/L. Here's a rough guide:

- **Low mineral content**: Less than 50 mg/L (e.g., Voss at 44 mg/L)
- **Medium mineral content**: 50-500 mg/L (e.g., Evian at 309 mg/L)
- **High mineral content**: Over 500 mg/L (e.g., Gerolsteiner at 2,479 mg/L)

Higher TDS generally means a more complex taste and higher mineral intake per serving.

### The Big Three: Calcium, Magnesium, and Sodium

These three minerals typically make up the bulk of a mineral water's dissolved content.

**Calcium (Ca)** is crucial for bone health. Waters like Gerolsteiner (348 mg/L) and San Pellegrino (185 mg/L) are considered calcium-rich. Drinking 1.5L of Gerolsteiner provides over 50% of your daily calcium needs.

**Magnesium (Mg)** supports over 300 enzymatic processes in your body, including sleep regulation and muscle recovery. Gerolsteiner leads here too with 108 mg/L — drinking 2L gives you about half your daily requirement.

**Sodium (Na)** is essential for fluid balance but is something many people want to limit. Most mineral waters are quite low in sodium compared to food sources. Even Gerolsteiner, at 118 mg/L, contains far less sodium per serving than a slice of bread.

### Other Important Minerals

**Bicarbonate (HCO3)** aids digestion and helps buffer acid in the body. High-bicarbonate waters (over 600 mg/L) like Gerolsteiner (1,816 mg/L) have been studied for digestive benefits.

**Sulfate (SO4)** supports liver and digestive function. San Pellegrino stands out with 476 mg/L of sulfate.

**Silica (SiO2)** is the beauty mineral — it supports collagen production and skin elasticity. Fiji water is famous for its high silica content at 93 mg/L.

**Potassium (K)** regulates heart rhythm and blood pressure. Most mineral waters contain modest amounts, typically under 10 mg/L.

### pH: What Does It Tell You?

The pH scale runs from 0 (very acidic) to 14 (very alkaline), with 7 being neutral. Most mineral waters fall between 5.5 and 8.5:

- **Acidic** (pH below 7): Perrier at 5.5, Topo Chico at 5.6
- **Neutral** (pH around 7): Smartwater at 7.0, Evian at 7.2
- **Alkaline** (pH above 7.5): Essentia at 9.5, Acqua Panna at 8.2

Sparkling waters tend to be more acidic due to dissolved CO2 forming carbonic acid.

### How to Choose Based on Labels

1. **For bone health**: Look for high calcium (over 150 mg/L) and magnesium
2. **For athletes**: High TDS with notable sodium and potassium
3. **For sodium-restricted diets**: Choose waters with sodium under 10 mg/L
4. **For skin health**: Look for high silica content
5. **For digestion**: High bicarbonate content helps
